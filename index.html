Deucy — Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 6, 2026 · Last updated: June 6, 2026

Deucy ("the app", "we", "us") is a tennis activity tracker. This policy explains what information the app accesses, how it is used, and the choices you have. Please read it together with Apple's own privacy terms for the App Store and Apple Health.

Contact: sofiyapukovska@gmail.com

The short version

  • Deucy is local-first: your sessions, posts, clubs, events, and profile are stored on your device. We do not operate a server that collects this data.
  • We access Apple Health, location, and your photo library only with your permission, and only to provide features you ask for.
  • We never sell your data, use your health data for advertising, or share it with data brokers.
  • You can revoke any permission at any time in iOS Settings, and delete your data by removing items in the app or uninstalling it.

Information the app accesses

Health & fitness data (Apple HealthKit)

With your explicit permission, Deucy reads workout, heart-rate, and calorie data from Apple Health — including tennis workouts synced from Apple Watch, Garmin, and Whoop — to log and display your tennis sessions automatically. With your permission, Deucy may also write tennis sessions back to Apple Health.

  • Health data is used only to show you your own activity inside the app.
  • Health data is never used for advertising or marketing, never sold, and never shared with third parties or used for any purpose unrelated to your tennis tracking, in accordance with Apple's HealthKit requirements.
  • Health data read from Apple Health stays on your device unless you choose to share a specific session.

Location

With your permission, Deucy uses your location to find tennis courts near you, and to record your on-court movement to generate a GPS "court coverage" heatmap for a session. Location is used while you are using the app; we do not continuously track your location in the background for any purpose other than an active session you start.

Photos and videos

With your permission, Deucy accesses your photo library so you can attach images and videos to your sessions, posts, clubs, and watch parties. Selected media is stored locally with the related item on your device.

Court information (Google Places)

When you browse courts, Deucy may fetch court details (name, address, ratings, reviews) from Google Places at view time. This information is displayed live and is not retained on our side beyond what is necessary to show it to you. Your use of Google-sourced content is also subject to Google's privacy policy.

Information you enter

Profile details, notes, scores, opponent names, coach names, and any activity links you paste (for example a shareable link from your health tracker) are stored locally on your device.

How information is stored

Deucy stores its data locally on your device using the operating system's standard app storage. The app does not require an account and, in its current version, does not upload your sessions, posts, or health data to a remote server controlled by us.

If a future version introduces optional accounts, cloud sync, or social features that transmit data off your device, this policy will be updated and you will be asked to consent before any such transfer.

Sharing features

When you choose to share a session, event, club, or watch party, Deucy generates an image card and/or a link that you send through the apps you select. Sharing happens only when you initiate it, and the content is sent through the channel you pick — we do not receive a copy.

Your choices and controls

  • Permissions: grant or revoke Health, Location, and Photos access anytime in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security (or Settings → Deucy).
  • Deleting data: delete individual sessions and posts inside the app. Uninstalling Deucy removes all locally stored app data from your device.
  • Health data: manage what Deucy can read or write in Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → Deucy.

Children's privacy

Deucy is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, with a notice in the app.
